Large-Scale Analysis of Gene Sensitivity - Individual project for Statistical Methods in Computer Science course. Used Broad Institute's L1000 dataset to identify genes that are most and least likely to change expression level under perturbation. This would be useful in development of new drugs, as if a gene changes expression level under a new drug, but not under many others, then it is likely significant to the drug's function. To accomplish this task, I used large scale ANOVA testing on the dataset with Benjaminiâ€“Hochberg correction for multiple testing. I considered this to be a two factor experiment, with perturbation being one factor and batch number another. See my full report here.
Predicting Smashbros - This was a group project for the class Supervised Machine Learning, where the goal was to predict the outcome of a game of Super Smashbros Melee given the current gamestate. In order to do this, data was collected from Project Slippi, a publicly available tool to record the gamestate at every frame of a game. We then cleaned the data and then used various algorithms to predict the outcome of various games given the current state of the game. Our best model achieved 74% accuracy, and an AUC of 0.83. However, measuring success in this project was very difficult, as it is more important that the model reflects the chances of each player to win the game than that the model predicts the outcome correctly. In the future, I'd like to add measurements for uncertainty of the prediction to this model.
Classifying Political Articles - This was a group project for the class Introduction to Data Management, where the goal was to find differences between news articles written from right, left, and center sources. To this end, news articles were scraped from various sites and classified under left, right, or center based on the site's political lean. We analyzed differences in the most used words in articles by source and by political lean, in addition to implementing machine learning algorithms to classify articles based on the article's text. A summary of the results can be found here.
Genre Classification by Lyrics - The goal of this project was to classify a song's genre based solely on its lyrics. Metrics for this project are a little complicated, and must be split up into two categories: success rate on songs that were not in the train set, but that were written by artists in the training set, and songs from artists that were not used for training. 72% accuracy was achieved on the former, and 62% on the latter. I believe better could be achieved with more training data, and potentially more complex approaches. My code can be found here, though it is disorganized and hasn't been touched in a long time.
